# 📚 Learning Resources

I'm creating this space to consolidate all the learning resources I've found genuinely useful on my data analytics journey.

We're living in a time where you can learn almost anything on YouTube — and the data analytics space has no shortage of content. I'm self-taught, learning at my own pace, and YouTube was the primary source that got me here.

---

## 🙋 A Little Backstory

We've all been there — enrolled in a course, watched 3 videos, then life happened. I have a graveyard of unfinished paid courses and honestly? No shame in that. They add value even when you don't finish them.

But the thing that really slowed me down was **tutorial hell**.

I was stuck in an endless loop of watching SQL course after SQL course without ever truly *understanding* SQL or actually practicing it. I kept consuming without building. Sound familiar?

That's when two resources changed everything for me — and that's really what this list is about. Not an exhaustive dump of every resource on the internet, but the ones that **actually moved the needle**.

---

## ⚡ What Actually Worked For Me

### 🎥 [Data With Baraa](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLNcg_FV9n7qZY_2eAtUzEUulNjTJREhQe)
`SQL` `Data Warehousing` `Projects`

> *"This is where SQL stopped being a subject and started being a tool."*

Baraa is an exceptional tutor with real industry experience behind him. If I had to describe his teaching style in one sentence — he goes under the hood. He doesn't just explain syntax; he explains the why behind every concept. And honestly, that's the right approach — syntax you can look up in the documentation anytime, but understanding how something actually works? That takes time and the right teacher. His hand-drawn sketches and animations are a bonus — they genuinely keep you glued to the screen.

---

### 🧠 [DataLemur](https://datalemur.com/sql-tutorial)
`SQL Practice` `Interview Prep` `Real Company Questions`

> *"Practice over passive watching — this is what broke the tutorial hell cycle for me."*

DataLemur is a bit different from everything else on this list — it's a place where you learn SQL by reading it. Sometimes sitting through a video feels like a task, and simply reading through concepts like a blog just hits differently. I honestly used to pull up DataLemur during lunch breaks or whenever I needed a few minutes away from work — it's that low-friction. If you're someone who wants to ease into SQL without committing to a full course, this is a great place to quietly get started.
